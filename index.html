Xunxu Duan, Hongbin Liu, Li Zhang Chuanmin Jia
Data Compression Conference 2024

Abstract

In recent years, artificial intelligence-generated content (AIGC) enabled by foundation models has received increasing attention and is undergoing remarkable development. Photo-realistic high-quality image generation can be elegantly realized with simple text prompts. Along with this feature, millions of AI-generated images (AIGI) have brought extreme bandwidth requirements in compression and transmission. However, the research on compression methods for AI-generated images is missing but necessary. To address this challenge, we begin our work as follows, we build the first AIGI dataset called PKU-AIGI-500K with diverse prompts associated with the generated images using five major foundation models. Based on this, we explore and analyze the essential characteristics of AIGC images, and empirically prove that existing data-driven lossy compression methods obtain sub-optimal or less efficient rate-distortion performance without fine-tuning due to a domain shift between AIGIs and natural ones. We thoroughly benchmark the rate-distortion performance and runtime complexity analysis of conventional and learned image coding solutions that are openly available, revealing new insights for emerging studies in AIGI compression.
